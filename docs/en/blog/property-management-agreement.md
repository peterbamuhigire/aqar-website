# Setting Terms: The Property Management Agreement

**Published:** 2025-05-22
**Author:** Peter Bamuhigire
**Featured Image:** Blog-Management-Agreement-HD.jpg
**Excerpt:** The success of property management hinges on one crucial document: the Property Management Agreement. Learn what essential clauses to include and how to protect your investment.

---

A property management agreement protects your income, defines responsibilities, and prevents disputes. Without one, both landlord and manager operate on assumptions — and assumptions cost money.

## Why a Weak Agreement Costs You Money

A thorough management agreement sets clear expectations, protects your rental income, and gives both parties a framework for accountability.

### Defining Roles and Responsibilities Clearly

- **Avoids Misunderstandings:** Without a clear agreement, both parties might operate under different assumptions about who is responsible for what. This can lead to neglected tasks, missed opportunities, and strained relationships.
- **Ensures Accountability:** The agreement clearly spells out the property manager's duties, making them accountable for specific actions and outcomes. This is crucial for evaluating their performance.
- **Sets Expectations:** It defines the level of service the owner can expect, from rent collection frequency to maintenance response times.

### Protecting Your Investment

- **Financial Safeguards:** The agreement should detail how your funds will be handled, preventing commingling of money and ensuring timely remittances of rental income.
- **Legal Recourse:** In the unfortunate event of a dispute or breach of contract, a strong agreement provides the legal framework for resolving issues and protecting the owner's rights.
- **Risk Mitigation:** By outlining responsibilities for areas like insurance, legal compliance, and emergency procedures, the agreement helps mitigate various risks associated with property ownership.

### Facilitating Smooth Operations

- **Standardised Procedures:** It outlines the processes for key management activities, ensuring consistency and efficiency in day-to-day operations.
- **Clear Communication:** It establishes communication protocols, including reporting frequency and methods, ensuring owners are kept informed about their property's performance.

## Essential Terms and Conditions

A comprehensive Property Management Agreement should cover every foreseeable aspect of the landlord-manager relationship.

### Appointment and Scope of Authority

This section formally appoints the property management company as the owner's exclusive agent and clearly defines the extent of their authority.

**Manager's Responsibilities:**
- Maximising occupancy based on current market conditions
- Collecting and enforcing collection of all financial dues, including security deposits and rental payments
- Advertising and promoting rental units in a reasonable and effective manner
- Maintaining accurate records of all funds received and disbursed
- Remitting collected net funds to the owner after deducting approved expenses and fees
- Providing an annual operating budget for the owner's approval

**Owner's Responsibilities:**
- Promptly furnishing the management company with all necessary documents and records
- Paying all property management fees as agreed
- Granting the management company authority to sign leases and set rental rates consistent with market conditions
- Authorising the management company to initiate legal proceedings to collect rent or evict residents

### Financial Management and Controls

This section is essential for ensuring clear financial oversight and protecting the owner's monetary assets.

**Insurance Obligations:** The management company should acquire and maintain insurance as selected by the owner. The owner is typically required to carry general liability insurance and name the management company as an additional insured.

**Bank Accounts (Trust Accounts):** The agreement must authorise the management company to establish and operate trust accounts for the property, ensuring all funds are deposited in conformance with applicable law.

**Operating Reserve:** The owner commits to maintaining sufficient operating funds to cover budgeted expenses and emergency costs as they arise.

**Resident Security Deposits:** The agreement should detail how security deposits are managed, including maintenance of a segregated account if required by law.

### Operational Management and Maintenance

**Repairs and Maintenance:** The management company is authorised to conduct ordinary repairs, replacements, and maintenance as reasonably necessary, on behalf of the owner and at the owner's expense. Owner approval is required for expenses exceeding a certain threshold unless it is an emergency.

**Utilities and Services:** The management company is authorised to negotiate and execute contracts for all necessary utilities and services. Contracts typically should not exceed one year.

**Employees:** The management company will employ and discharge all on-site personnel under its name but at the owner's expense.

### Administrative and Reporting Standards

**Administration and Record Keeping:** The management company must maintain complete records of all transactions relating to the property. These documents should be available for owner inspection at all reasonable times.

**Reports:** The agreement must specify the type and frequency of reports, including monthly income statements detailing gross revenues, expenses, compensation, and funds retained.

**Budgets:** The management company is responsible for preparing an annual operating budget for the owner's review and approval.

### Compliance and Legalities

The agreement should define permitted uses of the property and prohibit activities that could void insurance policies or violate governmental restrictions. It should address tax obligations, compliance with building codes, labour laws, zoning, and licensing requirements.

### Term, Termination, and Compensation

**Term:** The agreement specifies the initial term and usually includes automatic renewal clauses unless written notice is given by either party.

**Management Fee:** Typically defined as a percentage of gross revenues, payable in advance. Some agreements may include performance bonuses tied to achieving specific net operating income targets.

**Termination:** Conditions include expiration of term with proper notice, material breach with a specified cure period, or termination with or without cause upon giving specific notice. A cancellation fee may apply for premature termination without cause.

### Miscellaneous Provisions

- **Notices:** How and when official notices must be delivered
- **Modification:** Agreement can only be modified in writing
- **Governing Law:** Specifies which laws govern the agreement
- **Indemnification:** Defines limits of the management company's liability
- **Non-Solicitation:** Prevents the owner from hiring the company's key employees for a specified period

## Protect Your Investment With the Right Agreement

Choose your management partner carefully, and anchor that relationship with a thorough agreement. Clear terms, defined responsibilities, and transparent finances protect your property and your returns over the long term.
